Facility description

The Union Buildings, located in Pretoria, South Africa, are an architectural masterpiece and a symbol of the nation's government. Designed by Sir Herbert Baker and completed in 1913, the buildings serve as the official seat of the South African government and the office of the President. The structures, constructed from light sandstone, display a blend of neoclassical architecture with influences from Italian Renaissance and Cape Dutch styles. Set on Meintjieskop Hill, the Union Buildings span 285 meters in a curved crescent shape, offering panoramic views of the city below.


Surrounded by lush, well-maintained gardens and historic monuments, the Union Buildings are a popular site for both tourists and important national events. Among the notable features is a towering statue of Nelson Mandela, unveiled in 2013, symbolizing unity and the triumph of democracy in South Africa. The site has been witness to many significant moments in the country’s history, most notably Nelson Mandela’s inauguration as the first democratically elected president in 1994. Today, the Union Buildings remain a powerful symbol of South Africa's political landscape and its journey toward unity and equality.
